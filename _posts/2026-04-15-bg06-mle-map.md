How do we fit a model to data? The two most fundamental estimation principles are MLE and MAP.

## Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)

Given data $D = \{x_1, \dots, x_N\}$ and a parametric model $p(x \mid \theta)$, MLE finds:

$$
\theta_{\text{MLE}} = \arg\max_\theta \prod_{i=1}^N p(x_i \mid \theta) = \arg\max_\theta \sum_{i=1}^N \log p(x_i \mid \theta)
$$

The log-likelihood $\ell(\theta) = \sum_i \log p(x_i \mid \theta)$ is easier to optimize. For exponential family distributions, $\ell(\theta)$ is **concave**, guaranteeing a global optimum.

**Example — Gaussian MLE**:

$$
\mu_{\text{MLE}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N x_i, \quad \sigma^2_{\text{MLE}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N (x_i - \mu)^2
$$

Note: the variance estimate is **biased** (divides by $N$, not $N-1$).

## Maximum A Posteriori (MAP)

MAP adds a prior $p(\theta)$ via Bayes' rule:

$$
\theta_{\text{MAP}} = \arg\max_\theta \; p(\theta \mid D) = \arg\max_\theta \left[ \log p(D \mid \theta) + \log p(\theta) \right]
$$

The prior acts as a **regularizer**:
- Gaussian prior $p(\theta) \propto \exp(-\frac{\lambda}{2}\|\theta\|^2)$ → **L2 regularization** (Ridge)
- Laplace prior $p(\theta) \propto \exp(-\lambda \|\theta\|_1)$ → **L1 regularization** (Lasso)

## MLE vs MAP vs Full Bayesian

| Approach | Estimate | Computation |
|---|---|---|
| MLE | Point estimate $\hat{\theta}$ | Optimize $\ell(\theta)$ |
| MAP | Point estimate with prior | Optimize $\ell(\theta) + \log p(\theta)$ |
| Full Bayesian | Distribution $p(\theta \mid D)$ | Integrate (often intractable) |

As $N \to \infty$, MAP converges to MLE — the data overwhelms the prior.

## Cross-Entropy Loss = Negative Log-Likelihood

In classification, the **cross-entropy loss** is exactly the negative log-likelihood of the categorical distribution:

$$
\mathcal{L} = -\sum_{i=1}^N \sum_{k=1}^K y_{ik} \log \hat{p}_{ik}
$$

This means training a classifier with cross-entropy loss is performing MLE.
